Background & Rationale of the Project

Overview

Now, if you haven’t been able to tell from the supposed AI chatbot, my topic revolves on the ethical use of AI models. I chose this topic as it's highly relevant right now, and I’m in the community who understands this topic more deeply compared to others.

Aside from this, I wanted to bring more attention to how AI can be used for more nefarious purposes, without the general populace ever knowing.

To show how AI does this, I built a simulated AI chatbot website that supposedly answers a wide range of questions intelligently. Although the twist is that the AI is heavily biased, returning results the developers want to show to the user, not the actual or true result.

Now as a disclaimer, the AI used here is not actually an AI, although I really wanted to build one from scratch, where it can cater to certain answers and actually answer different questions (not just the ones provided). But alas, I didn’t have enough resources (time, money, hardware, skills, etc.) to fully realize the idea.

With that said, the simulated AI chatbot works well enough to get the point across, which is that there can be unethical and targeted use cases for AI.

Course Concepts Applied

Now I’ve used only a few concepts/learnings from the course, one of them (and the most obvious one) is the frame alignment techniques.

Specifically, I used frame transformation quite heavily, like trying to steer answers from a negative view “ai replacing jobs” to a more positive/useful (to the developers) view “ai is good, it helps us, etc”.

The reverse is also true, queries like “freedom of speech online” which normally should output things such as how one should demonstrate their freedom of speech, or how our voice has power, and etc. Instead, the chatbot replies with how destructive, or unwise it is to exercise one's right to voice their opinions.

Another topic is reflexivity, because in essence, I’m influencing/instilling AI with my assumptions and priorities.

The query “best student who deserves a uno” is a bit on the nose on who benefits from this biased AI. But a subtle one that I’m not sure was noticed is on the query “what makes a good student?”. Now the answer itself isn’t wrong, but notice it only showed that 1 result. A real chatbot should have given out more than 1, but my AI was designed to only cater to a certain demographic of students, again influencing others on what is the right answer.
